  • Pabbajjā: Ordination
    • background
      • During the early period ordination (pabbajjā) and higher ordination (upasampadā) were performed concurrently. Later on the Buddha declared them as two separate disciplinary acts.
      • Two types of monk status↓
        • sāmaṇera/ī
        • upasampadā
        • (for women there is also sikkhamāna in between sāmaṇerī and bhikkhunī)
    • sāmaṇera pabbajjā (ordination of novices)
      • how is the candidate for ordination called?→pabbajjāpekkha
      • three main sections and subsections of pabbajjā procedure. All three stages can be called pabbajjā:↓ #important
        • 1. Bhaṇḍukamma (shaving act)―
          • informing the saṅgha
          • shaving
          • bathing
          • requesting ordination
        • 2. Kāsāvacchādana (wearing the robes)―
          • giving the robes to the teacher
          • requesting the robes back
          • wearing the robes
        • 3. Saraṇadāna (undertaking refuge)―
          • requesting refuge and precepts
          • giving refuge and precepts
        • After ordination: requesting upajjhāya nissaya
      • Who can perform each step of the process of pabbajjā?↓ #important
        • informing the saṅgha: upasampanna or sāmaṇera following orders
        • bhaṇḍukamma: anyone, even the candidate himself but it is something that should be performed by the upasampanna
        • handing robes to the pabbajjāpekkha: the preceptor himself or someone else (bhikkhu, sāmaṇera or layperson) under the order of the preceptor
        • saraṇadāna: only done by a bhikkhu who knows the procedure well and can recite correctly (byatta paṭibala)―
          • The pabbajjācariya and the upajjhāya can be the same upasampanna or two different ones. If the pabbajjācariya is not the upajjhāya (does not have the qualities to be upajjhāya) he can only give pabbajjā {{6164101446998063::under the orders/for the sake of the upajjhāya}}
      • Compulsory factors for pabbajjā to be valid↓ #important
        • wearing robes having accepted them from the teacher (indirectly from a monk or sāmaṇera is also valid)
        • proper recitation of refuge by both the preceptor and the candidate (most basic factor)
          • If the robes are given from the candidate to the preceptor indirectly is it valid?→yes, it is valid

      • 2nd adinnādānā veramaṇī sikkhāpadaṁ samādiyāmi
        • 5 sambhāra↓ #important
          • 1. parapariggahitaṃ (An object that belongs to another human)
          • 2. parapariggahitasaññitā (perceiving the object as belonging to another human)
          • 3. theyyacittaṃ (intention to steal it)
          • 4. upakkamo (attempt on stealing it)
          • 5. tena haraṇaṃ (taking the object by that attempt)
        • 4 exceptions to stealing:↓ #important
          • sakasaññā (as belonging to oneself)
          • paṁsukūlasaññā (one perceives the object as discarded, ownerless)
          • vissāsaggāhī (taking on trust)
          • tāvakālikasaññā (taking temporarily)
        • There is no freedom to take other's properties because a loss has been cause to you. If a thief steals one's property, forcing it to be returned will break the precept but {{3977863993961184::if one still perceives the property as belonging to oneself}} one may get it back

        • ñatti sampatti / vipatti - informing the Saṅgha about the disciplinary act. It belongs to the kammavācā of the vinayakamma. There are 5 parāmasana:↓ #important
          • i. vatthu parāmasana - (vatthu refers to the candidate) The upasampadapekkha's name must be mentioned in the ñatti. Failing to do so is vatthu aparāmasana. Mentioning it as ‘ayaṃ nāgo’ is vatthu parāmasana.
            • parāmasanaṁ (nt.) √mas↔(+gen) touching (of); stroking (of); rubbing (of); caressing (of), contagion; tarnish
          • ii. saṅgha parāmasana - (saṅgha refers to the bhikkhus gathered for the disciplinary act) It should be stated as “suṇātu me, bhante saṅgho”. Failing to do so is aparāmasana. Mentioning it properly is parāmasana.
          • iii. puggala parāmasana - (puggala refers to the upajjhāya) The preceptor should be mentioned as “Nāgo tissassa upasampadāpekkho”. Failing to do so is aparāmasana. Doing it properly is parāmasana.
          • iv. ñatti parāmasana - Not stating the ñatti is ñatti aparāmasana. Stating the ñatti in the manner advised is ñatti parāmasana.
          • v. pubbe ñatti ṭhapana - ñatti should be stated prior to anusāvana (ñatti, anusāvana 1, anusāvana 2, anusāvana 3, conclusion). Not doing it in the proper order (doing anusāvana first and ñatti later) is pacchā ñatti ṭhapana (not valid). Doing it properly in order is pubbe ñatti ṭhapana.
